Day 4

The Little Things I Remember

This page is for the moments that may look small to the world, but never felt small to me.

Your good morning messages. The first message that comes from you in the morning. It tells me that somewhere, before the day gets too loud, you still remembered me.

Your random calls. Even a short call from you can change the way my whole day feels.

The way you hold my hand sometimes, even for a few seconds. I remember those seconds more than you know.

Your hugs. They are not small. They feel like shelter.

The first time you kissed my cheek. I did not know one small gesture could stay with someone for so long.

The way you understand my mood. The way you know when I am angry, when I am low, when I am hiding something, and when the child inside me needs softness more than logic.

Some people show love with big words. You show it in small actions that stay much longer.

A message I never forgot

There are a few lines from you that stayed with me because they made me feel that I was not imagining everything.

You once told me that you are emotionally attached to me. That when we do not talk, it affects you. That when my mood is bad, you feel like cheering me up.

You said the Delhi plan was a way for us to take some time, sit peacefully, roam around, talk properly, and just be together for a while.

You also said that even if life does not allow things to move ahead, the care and feeling in your heart would still remain.

I do not think you understood how much those words meant to me.

And then there was that softer message where you said sorry for hurting me, told me you knew I needed your time, and said you wished you could comfort me, make me rest, and gently calm me down.

That message felt like care. Not formal care. Real care. The kind that makes a difficult day feel lighter.

I also want you to know something I am proud of between us: I have always wanted you to feel safe with me. Every hug, every touch, every closeness has mattered to me only because it came with your comfort, your trust, and your consent.

That trust is precious to me. I never want to break it.

These little things are not little, Kanika Babu. They are the memories I carry quietly.

One answer, if you feel like saying something.
