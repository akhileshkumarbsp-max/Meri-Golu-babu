Day 7

You Matter To Me

Some people become important slowly. Not through one big moment, but through many small ones.

One message. One call. One small care. One moment where you noticed I was not okay.

Kanika, that is what happened with you.

You became important to me quietly, through all the small things that stayed.

The small things that stayed

The way you listen. The way you care and then pretend it was nothing. The way you get irritated and still come back. The way you called me for five minutes just to check on me.

The way you read something again and again, and then called me Dhakkan instead of simply saying you liked it.

The woman behind everything

Not just the Kanika who works. Not just the Kanika who manages things. Not just the Kanika who takes care of home, people, expectations, and responsibilities.

The Kanika who also gets tired matters to me. The Kanika who also needs care matters to me. The Kanika who should not be forgotten in between everything she does for everyone else matters to me.

I know I do not always say things properly. Sometimes it comes out with hurt, sometimes like a complaint.

But the truth underneath is simple: you have become very important to me.

You are seen here

You made me feel heard when I had stopped expecting that. You made me feel cared for in a way I had forgotten. You brought back softness in me.

Maybe that is why I keep trying to give you the same feeling back.

I want you to feel heard. I want you to feel seen. I want you to feel that someone notices the woman behind all the strength.

If this page has to say one thing

Kanika, you matter to me.

More than I say properly. More than I show calmly. More than I know how to handle sometimes.

And whatever happens, I will always be grateful that I got to know this version of you.

If this page made you feel anything, write one honest line here.

Thoda sa tum.

Thoda sa main.

Thoda sa hum.
