Day 7

The Things We Fight About Are Not Always The Real Things

Today I do not want this page to pretend that everything between us is always easy.

It is not.

We misunderstand each other. We fight over small things. Office. Lunch. Messages. Waiting. Time. A missed reply. A missed moment. A plan that does not happen.

And sometimes, from outside, maybe these things look small.

But they do not feel small to me when they are connected to you.

Because most of the time, I am not really fighting about that one thing.

I am fighting with the fear behind it.

The fear that maybe I do not matter as much as you matter to me. The fear that maybe I am asking for a place in your day that life does not easily allow. The fear that maybe something soft between us will slowly become distance.

And when that fear comes, I do not always speak properly.

Sometimes I taunt. Sometimes I sound angry. Sometimes I say things in a way that hurts you.

Sometimes my “I miss you” comes out as “you do not give me time.” Sometimes my “I needed you today” comes out like a complaint.

Sometimes my love becomes restless, and that is where I go wrong.

But the truth underneath it is simple.

I am not trying to fight with you.

I am trying, very badly sometimes, to say that you have become very important to me.

Maybe that is why small things hurt more than they should.

When You Say “Expectation”

There is one word that hurts me more than I show.

Expectation.

Sometimes when I ask for time, when I ask for a small moment, when I ask why something mattered to me, it becomes “expectation.”

And maybe from your side, it really feels like that.

Maybe you feel I am asking for something you cannot always give.

Maybe you feel tired because you already carry so much, and then my need also starts standing in front of you.

I understand that.

But from my side, it does not feel like a demand.

It feels like pain.

It feels like I am trying to say, “I need you a little today,” but it comes out badly.

It feels like I am trying to say, “Please make a little space for me too,” but it sounds like a complaint.

It feels like I am trying to say, “You matter too much to me,” but it becomes anger.

And yes, I will not lie.

I do have some expectations.

Because I care.

Because I love.

Because when someone becomes this important, you naturally start wanting a little time, a little softness, a little reassurance, a little place in their day.

I do not think that feeling itself is wrong.

What becomes wrong is the way I say it sometimes.

Sometimes I try to smile so you do not feel bad.

Sometimes I try to talk normally so the day does not become heavy.

Sometimes I try to control my anger because I know you are already going through a lot.

But inside, my mood breaks.

My day gets affected.

My eyes fill up.

And then the anger you see is not really anger.

It is hurt.

It is fear.

It is me not knowing where to keep all this feeling.

When I do something for you and you like it, it means something to me.

Not because I want something in return.

But because it tells me that somewhere, something from me reaches you.

And when that same space becomes “expectation,” I do not know how to hold it.

Because I am not trying to take from you.

I am trying to be there for you.

I am trying to make you feel seen.

I am trying to give you the care I feel you deserve.

And maybe in return, I just want to feel that I also have a small place somewhere in you.

Not a loud place.

Not a wrong place.

Just a real one.

So if we fight about expectations, maybe the real fight is not about expectation at all.

Maybe it is about two people who care, but do not always know how to carry that care without hurting each other.

You call it expectation.

I feel it as longing.

You feel pressure.

I feel distance.

You get tired.

I get scared.

And somewhere in between, both of us get hurt.

I do not want that.

I do not want my love to hurt you.

But I also do not want my pain to become invisible.

So I am writing it here, calmly, because I do not always say it calmly:

When I ask for time, I am not always asking for a plan.

Sometimes I am just asking to feel close to you.

When I get upset, I am not always angry at you.

Sometimes I am just scared of losing the softness between us.

And when I say something badly, the real sentence behind it is usually very simple:

I miss you, Golu Babu.

I miss you more than I know how to say properly.

Your side also matters

This page is not only about my pain.

Because I know you also have your side.

Your body pain. Your home. Your work. Your responsibilities. Your family. Your limits. Your own thoughts that you may not always be able to explain.

And maybe when I keep asking from my hurt, you feel tired too.

Maybe you feel helpless.

Maybe you feel like even when you care, you still cannot give what I am asking for.

Maybe you feel bad because you are also stuck between what you feel and what life allows.

I know this is not simple for you either.

That is why these fights hurt differently.

Because it is not like care is missing.

Care is there. Feeling is there. Softness is there.

But still, we hurt each other.

You hurt because you have limits. I hurt because I miss you.

You become quiet because you are tired. I become restless because I am scared.

You say “let's not discuss.” I hear it like distance.

I say something sharp. You feel I am not understanding.

And then both of us stand there with the same feeling, but on different sides of it.

What I want you to know

Kanika, you matter to me.

Not only on good days.

Not only when we are laughing.

Not only when you are soft.

You matter to me even when we misunderstand each other.

Even when you are tired.

Even when you are irritated.

Even when I am hurt.

Even when both of us do not know how to say things properly.

I do not want perfect.

I do not want easy.

I do not want a version of you that hides everything difficult.

I want to understand you.

And I want you to understand the pain behind my anger too.

Because most of the time, I am not angry because I care less.

I am angry because I care too much and do not know where to keep it.

So if we fight, I hope we still remember this:

There is care underneath.

There is love underneath.

There is fear underneath.

There is “I do not want to lose this” underneath.

And there is still us.

Kaisi adhuri ada… meri aankhon mein toh dekho.

Because sometimes I feel like the things I cannot say properly are still visible there.
