The Girl Who Asked Too Many Questions

The first thing I noticed about you was that you asked too many questions.

Honestly, it was irritating.

Every discussion had another question. Every integration had another doubt. Every process had another detail you wanted to understand.

I remember thinking: “Who is this girl? Will she even be able to handle Magic Checkout?”

At that time, I only saw the questions.

Later, I understood what those questions actually meant.

They meant you cared. They meant you wanted to understand. They meant you did not want to do things just for the sake of doing them.

You wanted to be good. Not average. Good.

The first thing I misunderstood about you later became one of the things I admired most.

And today, when I see how far you have come professionally, I feel genuinely proud.

Not surprised. Proud.

Because the girl who asked too many questions was never difficult. She was becoming excellent.

And I was too stupid to understand that at first.

You were never difficult, Kanika Babu. You were sincere. And sincerity looks confusing to people who are not paying enough attention.
