Day 6

The Place Where You Don't Have To Hide

Today, this page is not here to ask anything from you.

Not time. Not answers. Not explanations. Not promises.

Today, this page is just a small place where you can come and breathe for a minute.

Because I know you carry a lot

Your home. Your body. Your pain. Your thoughts. Your future. Your responsibilities.

And somewhere between all of this, you still try to care, reply, listen, and be present in your own way.

The part people sometimes forget

Sometimes I feel people see what you do, but they do not always see what it takes from you.

Coming back late from office, still thinking about home, cooking, washing, managing things, carrying responsibilities even on Saturdays and Sundays — and somewhere in between all of this, the person doing everything gets forgotten.

And Golu Babu, someone like you should not feel forgotten.

You should also be cared for. You should also be asked if you are tired. You should also be reminded that you matter — not only for what you do for everyone, but for who you are.

Maybe that is one thing I have always wanted to give you: the feeling that your effort is seen, your heart is seen, and the woman behind all the responsibilities is seen too.

I know there are parts of your life you cannot explain fully. I know there are things you carry quietly. I know there are boundaries, realities, responsibilities, and thoughts that are not easy to put into words.

And I don't want to force those words out of you.

I just want you to know that with me, you don't have to become perfect before you speak.

You don't have to arrange every sentence properly. You don't have to worry that I will judge you for feeling confused, tired, irritated, emotional, silent, or not okay.

Maybe sometimes your “hmm” is not an answer to decode.

Maybe it simply means: “I am tired. I am in pain. I don't have energy to explain. I am still here, but softly.”

I may not always react perfectly. Sometimes I get hurt. Sometimes I overthink. Sometimes I ask for more because I miss you more than I know how to say.

But beneath all of that, there is one truth that does not change:

I am on your side.

Today, when you video called me just to check how I was, it stayed with me.

It was only a few minutes. But sometimes a few minutes are enough to tell a person, “I noticed you were not okay.”

And Golu Babu, that matters to me more than I can explain.

You said small small things make you smile. And maybe that is exactly what this whole vault is.

Small small things. A line. A memory. A page. A photo. A stupid Dhakkan joke. A sentence like, “thoda sa tum ho, thoda sa main ho, thoda sa hum.”

Something you brought back

You brought back something I thought I had lost.

For a long time, I had started believing that maybe nobody would really understand me. Maybe I had to carry things alone. Maybe care, softness, and emotional safety were things other people experienced, not me.

Then you came into my life.

Not with big promises. Not with dramatic words. But through small things.

A message. A call. A few minutes just to check if I was okay. A line that made me smile. A small care that stayed longer than you probably realised.

One small memory of trust

There is one small memory I don't want to make loud, but I don't want to forget either.

That first cheek kiss from your side stayed with me differently.

Because for me, it was not about a moment. It was about trust.

It was that one small sign that you felt safe enough with me to be soft on your own.

No pressure. No demand. No asking. Just a small moment that said more than words could have said.

You are safe here

You know how I tell you things — my fears, my overthinking, my pain, my messy side.

And you listen. You respond. You don't make me feel small for feeling too much.

I want you to have that comfort with me too.

Not because you have to tell me everything. Not because I have any right to know every part of your life. But because I never want you to feel that you have nobody with whom you can keep even one honest piece of your heart.

Maybe I will not always have the perfect answer. Maybe I will not be able to solve everything.

But I can listen. I can try to understand. Maybe I can help you see one path when your mind feels too crowded.

And even if I cannot fix anything, I can still stay gentle with what you share.

I will not judge you. I will not make you feel small for being honest. I will not use your truth against you.

I may softly call you out when you pretend not to care. I may behave like a Dhakkan sometimes. But I will still be the person who wants to understand you gently.

I want to be a place you can come to

If there is something you are going through, I want to know — not to interfere, not to judge, and not to make you feel uncomfortable.

I want to know because I care about the person inside all those responsibilities.

I want to be someone you can allow into the parts of your world that you feel safe sharing — slowly, honestly, and without fear of being judged.

If you don't want to share something, I will respect it. But I also want you to know that if someday you feel like keeping even one small truth with me, I will hold it gently.

I am not asking for every detail of your personal life. I am only saying that you do not have to carry everything alone in front of me.

I want to be with you in whatever way is kind, safe, and possible.

You are important to me in a way I don't know how to measure.

You are not just someone I like talking to. You are someone whose mood changes my day, whose small care stays with me, whose pain I notice, and whose happiness I genuinely wish for.

And yes, I know this is not simple. I know there are things that cannot be changed just because we feel something. I know there are realities bigger than both of us.

But even inside all of that, I want you to feel one thing clearly:

You don't have to hide your heart from me.

What do you need from this page today?

You can open every answer behind every option. But what you choose first matters — that first choice will be saved softly for me, so I can understand what tomorrow should carry.

First choice saved softly ❤️
