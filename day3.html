Day 3

When You Became Important

I don’t think I realized it while we were talking every day.

I realized it when we stopped.

There was a time when communication between us slowed down or stopped properly. I do not remember the exact date or month last year.

But I remember the feeling.

Something felt missing.

At first, I thought it was normal. Maybe I was just missing a friend. Maybe I was just used to talking to you. Maybe it was just habit.

But it was not just habit.

Because the silence stayed. And I kept noticing it.

I did not realize you had become important when you were present. I realized it when your absence started feeling different.

That was probably the first time I understood that you had become important to me.

Not suddenly. Not dramatically. Quietly.

Somewhere between good morning messages, random calls, arguments, voice notes, care, fights, laughter, and all those small moments, you had become one of my favourite parts of the day.

And when that part became quiet, I felt it.

That is when I knew something had changed.
